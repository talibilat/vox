## What was done

Benchmarks (each 10 runs, medians and worst cases recorded in `docs/latency-spike.md`):

- faster-whisper tiny.en / base.en / small.en on a 10s clip.
- Piper vs Kokoro first-audio latency, paragraph and sentence-at-a-time.
- Silero VAD onset lag against a known onset position, plus per-chunk compute, summed against the 200ms interrupt budget.

Wake word: a first-pass "Hey Earshot" openWakeWord model was trained (features from openWakeWord's own pretrained backbone, small MLP head exported to ONNX, loaded and scored through `openwakeword.Model`).
Training data: 80 positive and 400 negative clips from 20 macOS `say` voices, augmented 5x.
Held-out test: 88 clips from 4 unseen macOS voices; final rates FN 1/8, FP 5/80 at the patience-4 / 0.95 operating point.
The trained model is committed at `spikes/models/hey_earshot.onnx`.

Debugging journey (details in the doc's "hard-won lessons"): batch-vs-streaming feature mismatch, dense-layer saturation on unnormalized embeddings, class-weight collapse, buffer-warmup out-of-distribution windows, and single-window vs patience scoring.
